Lupines

Lupines, big-leafed lupine, Lupinus polyphyllus, supposedly pronounced "LEW-pin" but everyone I know says "LEW-pine". Apparently named for a fang-like structure in the inner flower but I had heard that they were previously used to poison wolves.

I've just learned that the lupines of Minnesota's North Shore are invasive. Having recently moved here from the West where Lupinus is native and diverse, I was happy to see the big purple and pink masts in the ditches and puzzled over why they should exist out west and here but nowhere in between. Now I know that they were brought here as ornamentals and escaped into the margins.

Around Duluth I've not heard anyone talk of eradication, only appreciation. We'll be transplanting some into our gardens this summer.
